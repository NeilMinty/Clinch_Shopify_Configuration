Store Policies

Shopify surfaces policies at checkout, in the footer, and in customer account pages. All five standard policy pages need to be customised for Clinch IT's refurb context. The default Shopify templates are generic and legally insufficient for a refurbished goods retailer operating under UK consumer law.

Legal Note
UK Consumer Contracts Regulations 2013 apply in full to refurbished goods sold online. There is no reduced statutory right for pre-owned items. Customers have a 14-day right to cancel and return for any reason, starting from the day they receive the goods. Clinch IT's own 90-day warranty and battery exclusion policies apply from day 15 onwards and must not be written in a way that appears to limit the statutory 14-day cancellation right. This document is drafted accordingly. Independent legal review is recommended before go-live.

Policy pages are managed at: Settings → Policies

Policies are surfaced automatically in: checkout footer links, footer navigation (must be added manually to footer menu), customer account portal.

Clinch IT — Refund & Returns Policy

Your Right to Cancel (Statutory)

You have the right to cancel your order and return any item within 14 days of receiving it, for any reason, under the Consumer Contracts Regulations 2013. You do not need to give a reason.

To exercise this right, contact us within 14 days of delivery at vdavis@clinchit.com or call 020 3822 0855 (Option 3). Do not return an item before contacting us — we will provide a returns authorisation and instructions.

Once we receive and inspect the returned item, we will issue a full refund to your original payment method within 14 days. We may deduct from the refund if the item has been handled beyond what is necessary to establish its nature, characteristics, and functioning.

Returns Are Free

Returns within the 14-day statutory cancellation period are free of charge. We'll provide a prepaid return label — there's no cost to you for returning an item you've changed your mind about.

Items must be securely packaged for return. If you received the item in its original packaging, please reuse it. If the original packaging is damaged, use robust replacement packaging. For items containing batteries (including lithium batteries), packaging must be rigid and the item secured against movement. Damage caused by inadequate return packaging may affect your refund amount.

Faulty Items — Dead on Arrival (Days 1–14)

If your item arrives faulty or not as described, contact us immediately at vdavis@clinchit.com with your order number and a description of the fault. Faults reported within 14 days of delivery are covered under our Dead on Arrival (DOA) policy. We will arrange collection at our cost and offer a repair, replacement, or full refund at your preference.

Battery Faults — Important

Batteries are not covered under our standard 90-day warranty (see Warranty Policy). However, a battery fault reported within 14 days of delivery is treated as a DOA fault and covered at no cost to you. Battery faults reported after 14 days fall outside our warranty and will be assessed on a case-by-case basis.

Warranty Returns (Days 15–90)

Items that develop a fault after the 14-day cancellation period may be covered under our 90-day RTB (Return to Base) warranty. See our Warranty Policy for full details. Warranty returns require prior authorisation — contact us before sending any item back.

Non-Returnable Items

Items that have been damaged by misuse or accidental damage after delivery are not eligible for return or refund. Grade and cosmetic condition are clearly described at point of sale — cosmetic wear consistent with the stated grade is not a fault.

Inspection & Refund Timeline

Once we receive your return, we'll inspect it within 5 business days and process your refund or replacement. Refunds are issued to the original payment method. Card refunds may take 3–5 business days to appear depending on your bank.

Fraud Settings

Settings → Payments → Fraud prevention

Shopify Payments includes built-in fraud analysis scoring on every order. The following settings and thresholds are recommended for a refurb tech retailer. Thresholds are based on Shopify's published fraud prevention guidance and industry benchmarks for consumer electronics — a category with above-average fraud rates due to high resale value and frequent card-not-present abuse.

Basis for Recommendations
Thresholds below are derived from Shopify's fraud documentation and Signifyd's 2023 Consumer Electronics Fraud Benchmark Report, which identifies consumer electronics as a top-three category for CNP (card-not-present) fraud, with average order values in the £200–£600 range being highest risk. OD-C15 is removed — thresholds are set here as a recommended default and can be adjusted once trading data is available.

Core Settings

SettingRecommendationRationale
Fraud analysis Enable Shopify flags orders as Low / Medium / High risk automatically. Medium and High risk orders should not auto-fulfil.
CVV verification Require match — decline if CVV fails Standard requirement. Most legitimate buyers have the card in hand; a CVV failure on a high-AOV order is a strong fraud signal.
AVS (Address Verification) Flag mismatches for review — do not auto-decline AVS mismatches on their own are common (gift orders, moved house, business delivery addresses). Use as a review trigger, not a block.
3D Secure Leave enabled — do not disable Enabled by default with Shopify Payments. Shifts liability to the card issuer on authenticated transactions. Critical for high-AOV refurb tech.
Auto-fulfil on High risk Disabled Never auto-fulfil a High risk order. Manually review before dispatch.

Recommended Manual Review Thresholds

The following thresholds trigger a manual hold before fulfilment. They are recommended starting points — adjust after 60–90 days of trading based on actual order distribution.

TriggerThresholdAction
Shopify fraud risk score Medium or High Hold for manual review before fulfilling
Order value Above £400 Flag for secondary review — verify billing/delivery address match and check for velocity (same card, multiple orders)
Multiple orders, same card, within 24hrs 2 or more Hold all orders pending review. Card testing pattern.
Delivery address differs significantly from billing Different city or country Flag if combined with Medium/High risk score
EU orders above £200 £200 Lower threshold for EU orders given higher return complexity and no chargeback parity with UK cards
Note
The £400 UK threshold aligns with Clinch IT's likely AOV range for Grade A/B laptops and phones. At this value, a single fraudulent order represents material loss on a low-margin refurb unit. The EU threshold is set lower at £200 because cross-border chargebacks are harder to contest and return logistics are more complex. Review both after 60 days of live trading.

Shopify Admin — Fraud Review Workflow

When an order is flagged:

  1. Do not fulfil — leave order in Unfulfilled status
  2. Review the fraud analysis panel on the order: Orders → [Order] → Fraud analysis
  3. If satisfied, fulfil manually. If not, cancel the order and issue a full refund before the item leaves the building.
  4. Contact the card issuer if you suspect a compromised card — do not simply cancel without flagging.

Transactional Emails

Shopify sends transactional emails automatically based on order events. All templates are managed at: Settings → Notifications

Principles

  • All emails should reference the 90-day warranty and signpost the returns process — this is a key trust signal for refurb buyers
  • Battery exclusion should be visible in the order confirmation — not buried in policy pages
  • Tone: clear, direct, professional. Not warm or salesy. These are operational communications.
  • All emails must include the contact email and phone number — refurb buyers need to reach a human quickly if something is wrong
  • Branding (logo, colours) to be applied by the graphic designer — copy and structure below is configuration-ready
Note on Shopify Email Editing
Shopify notification templates use Liquid templating. Editing requires some familiarity with Liquid syntax or use of the built-in visual editor. Settings → Notifications → [Template name] → Edit code. For non-technical operators, editing the plain text fallback version is safer than editing the HTML.

Recommended Tools

Matrixify (Recommended for Bulk Import)

Matrixify (formerly Excelify) is the recommended tool for bulk product upload and ongoing stock management at volume. It allows product data to be managed in Excel or Google Sheets and imported directly into Shopify, including metafields, tags, and all standard product fields.

CapabilityDetail
Metafield supportFull — all custom metafields can be populated via Matrixify columns. This is the key advantage over Shopify's native CSV import, which does not support metafields.
Tag managementTags are managed as a comma-separated column in the import sheet. Bulk tag application and removal is supported.
Update without overwriteMatrixify can update specific fields on existing products without overwriting the whole record. Useful for price updates, condition updates, and inventory changes.
ExportFull export of Shopify product data to Excel. Use this to build a master stock spreadsheet that mirrors Shopify.
Error reportingImport errors are reported row-by-row so bad data is caught before going live.

Install: Shopify App Store → search "Matrixify". Free tier available. Paid tiers based on product volume — confirm which tier is appropriate based on Clinch IT's catalogue size.

Import template: Download Clinch IT Matrixify Import Template v1.1 (xlsx) ↓

When to Use Matrixify vs Manual Entry
  • Manual entry (Shopify admin): Adding 1–5 products at a time. Suitable for initial stock entry while learning the schema.
  • Matrixify: Adding 10+ products at once, or updating prices/conditions across multiple products simultaneously. The right tool for any volume operation.

Shopify Native CSV — Not Recommended for This Build

Shopify's built-in product CSV import does not support metafields. It can create basic product records but all metafield data (RAM, storage, battery health, condition grade, etc.) would need to be entered manually afterwards. For a metafield-heavy build like Clinch IT's, the native CSV is insufficient as a primary intake tool. It may be useful for initial product skeleton creation before metafields are populated.

Shopify Search & Discovery — Filter Health Check

After any bulk import, verify filter health in the Search & Discovery app: Apps → Search & Discovery → Filters. Check that new metafield values are appearing correctly as filter options. If a value is missing from a filter, it usually means the metafield was not populated consistently (e.g. "16 GB" instead of "16" for ram_gb).

Step-by-Step Intake Workflow

This is the repeating process for adding a new unit to Shopify. Follow this order — fields that drive automated collection rules and filters must be set correctly before the product is made Active.

Step 1 — Inspect and grade the unit

Before opening Shopify: run diagnostics, record battery health percentage, confirm all components present, assign condition grade (A/B/C), write condition notes. Have the serial number to hand.

Step 2 — Photograph the unit

Take real unit photos: hero image (square crop, white or neutral background), plus condition photos showing any cosmetic wear noted in condition notes. Do not use stock images for refurb units.

Step 3 — Create the product in Shopify

Products → Add product

Fill in: title (Brand + Model + Key Spec format), product type, vendor, price, compare-at price if applicable. Set status to Draft — do not set to Active until all fields are complete.

Step 4 — Upload images

Upload hero image first (Shopify uses first image as the collection card image), then condition photos. Add additional condition photos to the images_additional metafield if more than the main gallery.

Step 5 — Fill in all metafields

Scroll to the Metafields section on the product page. Fill in every required metafield for the product type. Use the pro forma tables above as the checklist. Do not skip warranty_months or charger_included.

Step 6 — Apply all tags

In the Tags field, add every required tag for this product type. Use the tag tables above. Key checks:

  • Grade tag matches condition_grade metafield
  • Battery band tag derived from battery_health_pct value
  • Screen size band tag derived from screen_size_inches (laptops)
  • Brand tag matches vendor field
  • OS tag present (laptops/desktops)
  • Network tag present (phones)

Step 7 — Set inventory

Set quantity to 1. Enable inventory tracking. Enter SKU.

Step 8 — Enter weight

Enter product weight in kg in the Shipping section. Required for EU calculated rates.

Step 9 — Preview and check

Before activating: use the product page preview to check the spec table displays correctly and condition notes are visible. Check the product would appear in the correct collection by verifying product type, vendor, and tags against the collection rules in the architecture document (§8).

Step 10 — Set to Active

Change product status from Draft to Active. The product will appear in all matching automated collections immediately. The eBay sync app will pick it up on its next sync cycle.

Step 11 — Verify in Search & Discovery

After activating, do a quick filter check on the relevant collection page to confirm the product appears in the correct filter results. If it doesn't appear under a filter it should match, the most likely cause is a metafield value inconsistency — check the value format against existing products.
